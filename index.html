Robotic Swarms

A Brief Survey of Problems and Solutions in Robotic Swarms
S.J.A. Bekhoven, S.P. Metman, M.J. Rogalla

Robotic Swarms are scalable networks of two or more robots, which have some sort of distributed intelligence.

That's great, let's actually see this in Action.

To be able to do all of this, we need to solve lots of problems.

Our Mission:

identify the problems and analyze the approaches which led to the solutions.

Main Problems

Exploration

Fully explore an unknown environment.

￬ ￪

Formation

Controlling the relative position and orientation of the robots in a group while allowing the group to move as a whole.

Dispersion

Achieve a static arrangement of elements that maximize the detection rate of targets appearing within the coverage area.

Source Localization

Fully explore an unknown area and find (multiple) source(s).
